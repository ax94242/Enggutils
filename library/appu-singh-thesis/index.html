Master's Thesis

Multi-scale computation approach using first principle theory for predicting crystal and electronic structure of doped Magnesium silicide
Full Text Available

The relatively inferior performance of magnesium silicide (Mg2Si) impedes its application in thermoelectric devices. In order to improve the thermoelectric properties of Mg2Si, the selection of doping elements and doping range has been reported in this work. Lower formation energies for the substitutional impurity of Al, Sn in Si and Na, Li in Mg sites have been suggested as n and p-type carrier types, respectively.

The characterization techniques like X-ray diffraction (XRD) and electron microscopy (TEM) analysis together with first-principles density functional theory (DFT) calculations confirmed the Mg2Si evolution with homogeneous doping of Al. The analysis showed that the 0.03 at% Al doping has the n-type semiconducting and the synergetic improvement in power factor (7.84 x 10^4 W/mK) with the significant reduction in the thermal conductivity (2.01 W/mK), thereby, achieved the superior Figure of merit (ZT) of 0.32 at 575 deg.C. The remaining elements, formation energy, variable cell relaxation, Mulliken charge, and transport properties calculation using the first principle based on DFT, were systematically investigated. Sn-doped in Mg2Si showed n and p-type semi conduction and achieved significant ZT value as p-type of 0.82 at 800 K with concentrations x= 0.6 and x = 0.4. At Sn concentrations x= 0.2 behavings as n-type, ZT reaches the maximum value of 0.33583 at 800 K because of the bi-polar nature of the compound. Li, Na doped in Mg2Si resulted in remarkable enhanced thermoelectric response in Mg2Si p-type with Li and Na doping was found to have abnormal behavior in crystal structure parameters. Abnormality mechanism is explained with Mulliken population analysis and supported with the density of states. With doping concentration for both Li and Na, x (at%) ~2, giving ZT value 0.76409 and 0.7697 at 400 K. For further enhancement of ZT value, Mg2Si p-type found with Na, an attempt was made to dope Li in the range of 2<x<4.1 at % with Mg1.98SiNa0.02 but showed lower thermoelectric performance in the considered range.
