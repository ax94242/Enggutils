Transparency reporting requirements in Healthcare

What is a transparency report in Healthcare?

A transparency report contains the information about the relationship between the Healthcare companies and Healthcare professionals (HCP) and Healthcare organizations (HCO). Majority of the transparency report focusses any form of Transfer of Value (ToV) provided to the benefit of Healthcare professionals (HCP) and Healthcare organizations (HCO) by Healthcare companies.

A Transparency reporting requirements can be set up by companies business geo-located statutory bodies, regulatory bodies or may be the voluntarily joined local association. While transparency report requirement from a statutory bodies or a regulatory bodies is a legal obligation to fulfill mandatorily, whereas the association- business ethics code is an optional. A transparency requirement from the voluntarily joint association will only be required when the company has an active membership else not required.

Why transparency report exist?

With realized potential of transparency reports, more and more different geo-located statutory bodies, regulatory bodies, or local associations are now setting up requirement almost every year.
Transparency report can help provide insights on healthcare business and growth. Some of the questionnaries, it helps you answer are:
1. Why Healthcare cost is so high? What is the root cause?
For an instance, most of the reported healthcare increments are due to the sales and marketing activity [1]. Transparency report intends to capture all such practice.
2. What is the Healthcare research growth and advancement in the region?
3. Are Heathcare companies practicing sales related interaction ethically in the region?
